It all started in 1887

Sports in Pittsburgh have been played dating back to the American Civil War. Baseball, hockey, and the first professional American football game had been played in the city by 1892. Pittsburgh was first known as the "City of Champions" when the Pittsburgh Pirates, Pittsburgh Panthers, and Pittsburgh Steelers won multiple championships in the 1970s.


Pittsburgh Steelers

The Pittsburgh Steelers were founded by Arthur J. Rooney on July 8, 1933. Now the seventh-oldest franchise in the NFL, the Pittsburgh team was known as the Pirates until 1940. Most recently Mike Tomlin led the team to victory in Super Bowl XLIII. The win marked their sixth championship in franchise history as the Steelers became the first team to win six Super Bowls.


Pittsburgh Pirates

Founded as part of the American Association in 1881 under the name Pittsburgh Allegheny, the Pittsburgh Pirates joined the National League in 1887 and was a member of the National League East from 1969 through 1993. The Pirates are now a part of the NL Central Division and have five World Series wins.
